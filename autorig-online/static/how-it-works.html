The Auto Rigging Process

Auto rigging is an automated pipeline: your model is analyzed, a skeleton is fitted, skin weights are applied, animation-ready exports are generated, and the final files are delivered. The goal is a fast, repeatable workflow that gets you from "static mesh" to "animated character" with minimal friction.

1

Upload Your Model

Start by uploading your GLB, FBX, or OBJ file — or paste a direct download link. For best results, use a single character mesh in a clean T-Pose (arms straight, neutral stance).

2

Model Analysis (Structure & Proportions)

Next, the system examines your character's shape and proportions to understand where joints should be placed and how the mesh should deform. The better the input (pose, symmetry, clean geometry), the smoother the final rig.

What matters most at this stage:
• Clear silhouette and symmetry
• Reasonable human proportions (for humanoid rigs)
• Clean mesh (avoid broken normals, non-manifold geometry if possible)
3

Skeleton & Bone Hierarchy Generation

Once the character structure is understood, a skeleton is created: a bone hierarchy aligned to the body, with joint placement optimized for natural movement. This becomes the foundation for animation, retargeting, and engine import.

Typical output includes:
• A humanoid bone structure
• A stable hierarchy suitable for animation playback
• Consistent alignment for exports
4

Skinning (Weights That Make It Move)

A skeleton alone isn't enough — the mesh must be attached to the bones using skin weights. This step determines how cleanly shoulders, elbows, knees, and hips deform during animation.

Good results depend on:
• Clean topology around joints
• Symmetrical pose
• Avoiding extreme armor-like geometry (or expecting minor artifacts)
5

Animation Integration (50+ Animations)

After the rig is ready, the character is packaged with an animation set (walk/run/idle and more). This is where you go from "rigged" to "actually useful in a project."

You'll receive:
• Animated exports for common workflows
• A quick preview video of the result (_video.mp4) for sharing and fast review
6

Quality Check & Download

When the job completes, you'll see downloadable links. Progress is calculated from the number of output files already available for download — as files become ready, progress increases. This means you see real progress, not estimates.

Powered by a Practical Automated Pipeline

The goal is consistent, production-friendly results — not a pile of settings. The automated rigging pipeline focuses on three core capabilities:

Automatic Skeleton Fitting

The system identifies structure (limbs, torso, proportions) to place joints where movement should happen.

Procedural Rig Construction

A consistent rig hierarchy is generated so exports behave predictably across models and projects.

Automatic Skinning

Weights are produced to achieve usable deformations quickly, especially for common humanoid movement.

Traditional Rigging vs Auto Rigging

Manual rigging is powerful, but slow and expensive when you're iterating or working on many characters. Auto rigging is the fastest way to go from "model" to "animated testable character" — especially for prototypes, NPCs, and content pipelines.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take?

Typical jobs finish in ~5–15 minutes depending on complexity and current server load. Progress is calculated from the number of output files already available for download, so you see real progress, not estimates.

Why T-pose?

T-pose provides optimal compatibility with animation systems, motion capture data, and game engines. It ensures clean skeleton fitting and proper skinning weights.

What do I download?

You'll receive rigged + animated GLB, Unity FBX, Unreal FBX, and a preview video (_video.mp4). All files are engine-ready and organized for immediate use.

Do I need Blender/Maya?

No. The service works entirely in your browser. Upload your model, wait for processing, and download the results. No 3D software installation required.

What models don't work well?

Extremely stylized characters, non-humanoid shapes, or models with broken topology may produce minor artifacts. Auto rigging is a fast baseline — hero characters may still need manual polish for perfect deformation.

Notes for Best Results

  • ✓ Use a true T-Pose (symmetry matters)
  • ✓ Keep one character per file when possible
  • ✓ If the mesh is extremely stylized, expect small artifacts — auto rigging is a fast baseline, and hero characters may still need polish
